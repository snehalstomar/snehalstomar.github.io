stomar@cs.stonybrook.edu

Hello! I am a first year CS Ph.D. student at Stony Brook University. I am interested in Deep Generative Models for Computer Vision applications. I also enjoy working on problems in Computational Photography and 3D Computer Vision. I also serve as an AI evangelist at SolveitNOW, a startup that is revolutionizing peer learning for K-12 students. Previously, I was a pre-doctoral fellow with the Visual and Embodied AI group at TCS Research, working on efficient 3D mesh animation using Self-Supervised Graph Neural Networks. Before that, I graduated from the Indian Institute of Technology Madras with an MS (by Research) in Electrical Engineering, having received the Institute Research Award for excellence in research. There, I was a part of the Image Processing and Computer Vision (IPCV) Lab, advised by Prof. A.N. Rajagopalan.

Even before that, I did several research internships with Prof. Shubhendu Bhasin at IIT Delhi as an undergrad working in the domain of Control Systems and Robotics. I was also a part of my university’s AI Robotics Team where we worked on building an autonomous car.
